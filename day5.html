Hakone Shrine & Lake Ashi



Hakone Shrine is famous for its iconic red torii gate standing majestically in the waters of Lake Ashi. This sacred Shinto shrine, founded in 757 AD, offers one of Japan's most photographed views with Mount Fuji as a backdrop.

Lake Ashi is a stunning crater lake formed by volcanic activity 3,000 years ago. The lake's serene waters perfectly reflect the surrounding mountains and sky, creating picture-perfect moments throughout the day.

‚õ©Ô∏è The Famous Torii

The 18-meter tall vermillion torii appears to float on Lake Ashi's waters. Originally built in 1667, it marks the boundary between the physical and spiritual worlds. At high tide, it seems to rise from the lake itself.

üóª Mount Fuji Views

On clear days, Mount Fuji provides a magnificent backdrop to the torii gate. Early morning hours (6-8 AM) offer the best visibility. The lake's calm waters create perfect reflections of both mountain and gate.

üõ∂ Pirate Ship Cruises

Take a replica pirate ship cruise across Lake Ashi for unique views of the torii from the water. The 30-minute journey offers panoramic mountain views and photo opportunities from different angles.

üå∏ Seasonal Beauty

Each season offers different beauty: spring cherry blossoms, summer green forests, autumn maple colors, and winter snow-capped mountains. Late November features stunning autumn foliage around the lake.

Hakone Open-Air Museum



Hakone Open-Air Museum is Japan's first outdoor sculpture museum, featuring over 120 masterpieces displayed against the stunning backdrop of Hakone's mountains. Art and nature blend seamlessly in this unique cultural experience.

Founded in 1969, the museum showcases works by renowned artists including Picasso, Henry Moore, and Rodin. The 70,000 square meter park allows visitors to interact with art while enjoying the therapeutic benefits of nature.

Hakone Ropeway Adventure



Hakone Ropeway is one of Japan's most scenic cable car rides, soaring over the volcanic Owakudani Valley and offering spectacular views of Mount Fuji, Lake Ashi, and the surrounding mountains.

The 4-kilometer journey takes you from Sounzan to Togendai, passing over active volcanic vents and sulfur springs. On clear days, the panoramic views are simply breathtaking, making it a must-do Hakone experience.

The highest point of the ropeway offers unobstructed views of Mount Fuji on clear days. Early morning rides provide the best visibility before clouds form around the mountain.

üåã Volcanic Valley Views

üì∏ Photography Tips

Owakudani Volcanic Valley



Owakudani Valley is an active volcanic zone created by a volcanic eruption 3,000 years ago. Known as the "Great Boiling Valley," it features dramatic sulfur vents, hot springs, and the famous black eggs cooked in the volcanic waters.

The otherworldly landscape with its sulfur-yellow rocks, steaming vents, and constant volcanic activity offers a fascinating glimpse into the geological forces that shaped this region. It's said that eating one black egg extends your life by seven years!

ü•ö Famous Black Eggs

How They're Made

Regular chicken eggs are boiled in the naturally hot volcanic springs at 80¬∞C for 60 minutes. The sulfur and iron in the water react with the eggshell, turning it completely black while the inside remains a normal white and yellow.

The Legend

Local legend claims that eating one black egg adds seven years to your life. While scientifically unproven, the tradition has made these eggs one of Japan's most famous local delicacies.

Taste & Experience

Despite their dramatic appearance, black eggs taste exactly like regular hard-boiled eggs. The fun is in the unique experience and photo opportunity rather than any special flavor.

üí® Active Sulfur Vents

Steam continuously rises from dozens of vents throughout the valley. The sulfur smell is strong but not dangerous. The vents reach temperatures of over 100¬∞C, hot enough to cook eggs!

üèúÔ∏è Lunar Landscape

The barren, rocky terrain colored by sulfur deposits creates an otherworldly atmosphere. The landscape is so unique that it's often compared to the surface of the moon or Mars.

‚ö†Ô∏è Safety & Monitoring

Hakone's volcanic activity is constantly monitored. The area may close when volcanic gas levels rise too high. Stay on designated paths and follow all safety guidelines during your visit.

üõçÔ∏è Souvenir Shopping

The visitor center offers unique volcanic-themed souvenirs including sulfur soaps, black egg-themed snacks, and geological specimens. Perfect for bringing home a piece of this unique experience.
