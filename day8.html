Osaka Castle



Osaka Castle is one of Japan's most famous landmarks and a symbol of Osaka. Originally built in 1583 by Toyotomi Hideyoshi, the current reconstruction offers stunning views and fascinating history.

The castle sits in beautiful Osaka Castle Park, surrounded by moats and featuring over 600 cherry trees. The museum inside houses artifacts from the castle's turbulent history and Japan's unification period.

Sumiyoshi Taisha Shrine



Sumiyoshi Taisha is one of Japan's oldest and most important Shinto shrines, with over 1,800 years of history. It's famous for its unique architectural style that predates Chinese influence on Japanese temple design.

The shrine complex features distinctive curved bridges, vermillion buildings, and ancient trees. It's dedicated to sea gods and has been protecting sailors and travelers for centuries.

Shinsekai District



Shinsekai (New World) is Osaka's retro entertainment district, famous for kushikatsu restaurants, vintage arcade games, and the iconic Tsutenkaku Tower. It perfectly captures old Osaka's working-class spirit.

Built in the early 1900s to showcase "the world of the future," Shinsekai now feels charmingly nostalgic. The neon signs, vintage pachinko parlors, and traditional eateries create a unique atmosphere found nowhere else.

National Bunraku Theatre



Bunraku is traditional Japanese puppet theater that originated in Osaka and is recognized by UNESCO as a Masterpiece of Oral and Intangible Heritage. This highly sophisticated art form combines storytelling, music, and intricate puppetry.

Each puppet is operated by three skilled puppeteers working in perfect harmony. Combined with shamisen music and dramatic narration, bunraku creates an emotionally powerful theatrical experience that has captivated audiences for over 400 years.
