Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan



Osaka Aquarium Kaiyukan is one of the world's largest public aquariums, famous for its massive central tank housing whale sharks. The aquarium recreates the Pacific Ring of Fire with habitats from around the Pacific Rim.

The unique spiral-shaped building allows visitors to descend through 8 floors, observing marine life from different depths and perspectives. The centerpiece 5,400-ton Pacific Ocean tank is truly breathtaking.

Tempozan Giant Ferris Wheel



Tempozan Giant Ferris Wheel stands 112.5 meters tall and was once the world's largest Ferris wheel. It offers spectacular 360-degree views of Osaka Bay, the city skyline, and on clear days, even Mount Fuji.

The wheel features 60 gondolas and takes about 17 minutes for a complete rotation. At night, the wheel becomes a giant illuminated landmark with LED displays that can be seen from across the bay.

Namba Parks



Namba Parks is an innovative shopping complex featuring a spectacular multi-level rooftop garden that creates a "canyon" effect. This award-winning architectural marvel combines retail, dining, and nature in downtown Osaka.

The 8-level rooftop park features waterfalls, cliffs, lawns, and over 300 varieties of trees and plants. It's a green oasis in the urban jungle, offering a unique shopping and relaxation experience.

Okonomiyaki Tasting



Okonomiyaki is Osaka's signature dish - a savory pancake that literally means "grilled as you like it." This beloved comfort food represents the soul of Osaka's food culture and working-class spirit.

Visit authentic okonomiyaki restaurants in Namba or try making your own at interactive cooking experiences. Learn about the history, regional variations, and proper eating techniques of this iconic dish.

ğŸ“œ Historical Origins

Born during WWII food shortages, okonomiyaki was a creative way to make filling meals from limited ingredients. It evolved from simpler flour-based dishes and became Osaka's symbol of resilience and creativity.

ğŸ¥¬ Ingredients & Variations

The base includes flour, eggs, shredded cabbage, and dashi. Popular additions include pork belly, seafood, cheese, or mochi. Each region has its style - Osaka's is mixed together, while Hiroshima's is layered.

ğŸ½ï¸ Eating Etiquette

Use the small metal spatula to cut portions, then pick up with chopsticks. Don't cut the entire pancake at once - it's considered rude. The communal cooking experience is as important as the taste!

Umeda Sky Building



Umeda Sky Building is an architectural masterpiece featuring a unique "floating" observatory connecting two towers. This futuristic landmark offers some of the best panoramic views of Osaka and unforgettable sunset experiences.

The building's innovative design includes escalators that transport visitors through a tunnel of lights to the circular observation deck 173 meters above ground. It's particularly magical during evening hours when the city lights begin to sparkle.
